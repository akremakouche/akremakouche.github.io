Fixed Point Iteration and Newton-Raphson Hybrid Method






Diffinition:

The Newton-Raphson/Fixed Point Hybrid Method is a numerical technique that integrates the strengths of both Newton-Raphson and Fixed Point Iteration for solving nonlinear equations. It utilizes Newton-Raphson's derivative-based rapid convergence while leveraging Fixed Point Iteration's stability. By alternating between these two methods, the hybrid approach enhances reliability, particularly in cases where Newton-Raphson may struggle due to small derivatives or poor initial guesses. This combination leads to improved accuracy and consistent convergence toward the root of the equation. The method is particularly effective in dealing with challenging functions.
